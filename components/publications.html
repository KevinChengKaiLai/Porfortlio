LLM-guided Chemical Process Optimization with a Multi-Agent Approach

Tong Zeng, Srivathsan Badrinarayanan, Janghoon Ock, Cheng-Kai Lai, Amir Barati Farimani

Department of Chemical Engineering, Carnegie Mellon University

This work addresses the constraint definition bottleneck in chemical process optimization by presenting a multi-agent framework of large language model (LLM) agents that autonomously infer operating constraints from minimal process descriptions. The AutoGen-based framework employs OpenAI's o3 model with specialized agents for constraint generation, parameter validation, simulation execution, and optimization guidance. Validated on the hydrodealkylation process, the framework achieved a 31-fold reduction in wall-time relative to grid search, converging in under 20 minutes while maintaining competitive performance with conventional methods (IPOPT and grid search).

Co-first author, Unraveling the Alternative Process Configurations for More Environmentally Friendly Maleic Anhydride Production

Ting-Yu Yu, Ching-Tai Kuo, Cheng-Kai Lai ,Wangyun Won, Bor-Yih Yu

Process System Engineering Laboratory, National Taiwan University

This study proposes and evaluates alternative configurations for producing maleic anhydride (MA) through n-butane oxidation to enhance environmental friendliness. Using advanced numerical modeling with NumPy/SciPy, we achieved 97.52% energy efficiency and 99.9% purity. Our optimized configuration demonstrates superior environmental and economic performance compared to existing literature, with significant potential for industrial implementation.
